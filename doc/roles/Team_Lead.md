# Team Lead

## Role Overview

The Team Lead is a junior people manager responsible for the
day-to-day leadership of an engineering team. The role carries
the same broad scope as a Software Engineering Manager —
covering people, delivery, technical oversight, process, and
cross-functional collaboration — but the Team Lead is earlier
in their management career and is expected to be developing
their skills and instincts in each of these areas rather than
operating with full independence. A Team Lead works with closer
support from their manager than a fully seasoned Engineering
Manager would require, and is not yet expected to handle every
situation with the same confidence or consistency.

---

## Responsibilities

### People Leadership & Team Health

- Works to build relationships with direct reports through
  regular one-on-ones, with a focus on support and open
  communication.
- Provides feedback to team members and is developing the
  skill of making that feedback timely, constructive, and
  effective.
- Begins to identify growth opportunities for team members and
  works toward developing career progression plans with
  guidance.
- Manages straightforward performance situations and escalates
  more complex ones to their manager as needed.
- Contributes to a positive team culture, including a sense of
  psychological safety and continuous improvement.

### Delivery & Execution

- Takes responsibility for the team's delivery and works to
  develop the habits and systems needed to deliver software
  reliably and at a good standard of quality.
- Monitors progress against commitments and attempts to
  identify and address risks to delivery, escalating where
  uncertain.
- Works to ensure the team has clear priorities and is
  protected from unnecessary interruptions, with support from
  their manager.
- Addresses process and organizational blockers where able,
  and escalates promptly when they are beyond their authority
  or experience to resolve.
- Maintains visibility into team workload and capacity, and is
  developing the judgment needed to adjust plans in response.

### Technical Oversight

- Maintains a working understanding of the team's systems
  sufficient to contribute to decisions about scope, staffing,
  and risk.
- Encourages the team to adhere to engineering standards and
  sound development practices, and is developing the authority
  and confidence to uphold them.
- Works with senior engineers to surface and manage technical
  debt and systemic risks, and is learning to assess these
  independently.
- Supports sound technical decision-making within the team,
  leaning on senior engineers and their own manager where
  guidance is needed.
- Shows genuine care for engineering quality, maintainability,
  and reliability, and promotes these as team values.

### Process & Ways of Working

- Helps establish and maintain the team's development
  processes, and looks for opportunities to improve them over
  time.
- Runs or facilitates retrospectives and other feedback
  mechanisms, and is developing the skill of translating that
  feedback into meaningful change.
- Works to reduce friction in day-to-day workflows, with a
  developing sense of which process overhead is necessary and
  which is not.
- Encourages good documentation practices and knowledge
  sharing within the team.

### Cross-Functional Collaboration

- Serves as the engineering point of contact for the Product
  Manager, UX, and other stakeholders, and is developing the
  confidence and effectiveness to do this independently.
- Participates in roadmap planning and works to represent
  engineering constraints and capacity accurately.
- Communicates team progress, risks, and decisions to
  stakeholders, and is developing the judgment to calibrate
  what needs to be shared and when.
- Coordinates dependencies with other engineering teams, with
  support from their manager for more complex or sensitive
  situations.

### Strategy & Organizational Contribution

- Begins to contribute perspectives on engineering standards,
  culture, and tooling at the team and department level.
- Is developing the organizational awareness needed to
  represent the team's needs effectively in broader forums.
- Takes an interest in the wider engineering organization and
  looks for opportunities to contribute beyond the immediate
  team.

---

## Key Skills & Competencies

- Technically credible with peers; earns respect as an
  engineer while growing into a management identity.
- Developing people management skills; shows genuine interest
  in the growth and wellbeing of direct reports.
- Communicates clearly and is working toward the directness
  and confidence needed for difficult conversations.
- Reliable and organized; follows through on commitments and
  keeps relevant people informed.
- Open to coaching and feedback from their manager, and
  actively works to apply it.
- Beginning to balance team-level concerns with individual
  engineering work, where applicable.

---

## Interfaces With

| Role | Nature of Interaction |
| --- | --- |
| Engineering Team | Day-to-day leadership, coaching, and unblocking |
| Engineering Manager / Director | Regular support, coaching, and escalation |
| Product Manager | Shared planning, prioritization, and delivery coordination |
| UX / Design | Coordination on feasibility and implementation approach |
| Senior Engineers | Collaboration on technical quality and decision-making |
| Other Team Leads / Managers | Cross-team coordination and peer learning |
| HR / People Team | Hiring support, performance management, and employee relations |

---

## What Success Looks Like

- The team delivers reasonably consistently and the Team Lead
  is developing the habits and systems to improve that over
  time.
- Direct reports feel supported and receive useful feedback,
  even if its delivery is still being refined.
- Stakeholders have a reasonable level of confidence in the
  team, and the Team Lead is building toward the trust that a
  more experienced manager would command.
- The Team Lead identifies and escalates risks and issues in a
  timely way, and is developing the ability to resolve more of
  them independently.
- The Team Lead is visibly growing as a manager — taking on
  more, leaning on their manager less, and handling a broader
  range of situations with increasing confidence.
- The team is in a better position because of the Team Lead's
  involvement, even where that involvement is still
  developing.

## Comparison with Software Engineering Manager

The Team Lead and the Software Engineering Manager perform the
same fundamental job, but the Engineering Manager is expected
to do it with greater independence, consistency, and
effectiveness.

| | Team Lead | Software Engineering Manager |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Scope** | Single engineering team | Single engineering team |
| **Responsibilities** | The same broad set as an EM | People, delivery, technical oversight, process, and collaboration |
| **Independence** | Works with closer support from their manager | Operates independently across all areas of the role |
| **People management** | Developing; handles straightforward situations, escalates complex ones | Fully capable; handles the full range of performance and people situations |
| **Delivery accountability** | Takes responsibility and is developing the tools to meet it | Fully accountable for team delivery |
| **Stakeholder communication** | Developing confidence and judgment | Fully independent and trusted |
| **Expected trajectory** | Growing toward full EM effectiveness | Operating at full EM effectiveness |

The Team Lead is not a lesser version of a different job — it
is an earlier stage of the same job, with the expectation that
they are actively developing toward the full effectiveness of a
Software Engineering Manager.
