# VP of Software Engineering

## Role Overview

The VP of Software Engineering is the senior leader responsible for the entire engineering organization. They set the long-term technical and organizational direction for engineering, ensure the function is appropriately structured and resourced, and serve as the primary voice of engineering at the executive level. The VP is accountable not just for what engineering delivers today, but for building the organization's capability to deliver effectively over time. This role requires equal parts technical credibility, business acumen, and organizational leadership.

---

## Responsibilities

### Engineering Strategy & Vision
- Defines the long-term vision and strategy for the engineering organization, in alignment with company objectives.
- Identifies the capabilities, technologies, and organizational investments needed to support the company's future direction.
- Makes high-stakes decisions about engineering architecture, platforms, and foundational infrastructure with a multi-year perspective.
- Stays current on industry trends and emerging technologies, incorporating relevant developments into the organization's direction.

### Organizational Leadership & Culture
- Leads and develops a team of Directors and senior engineering leaders.
- Sets the cultural tone for the engineering organization — establishing values around quality, ownership, collaboration, and continuous improvement.
- Builds and maintains a high-performing, diverse engineering organization at scale.
- Fosters an environment where engineers at all levels are engaged, challenged, and growing.
- Addresses organizational dysfunction or cultural issues decisively and constructively.

### Delivery & Accountability
- Holds the engineering organization accountable for delivering reliably against the company's product and technology roadmap.
- Maintains a clear picture of delivery health, risk, and capacity across all engineering teams.
- Ensures systemic issues — in process, tooling, staffing, or architecture — are identified and resolved before they become critical.
- Balances the pressure to ship with the need to invest in engineering foundations and long-term sustainability.

### Headcount, Budget & Resource Allocation
- Owns the engineering headcount plan and budget, and makes high-level resourcing decisions.
- Partners with Finance and HR on workforce planning, compensation strategy, and organizational structure.
- Allocates engineering capacity across the portfolio of work in alignment with company priorities.
- Makes build vs. buy decisions for significant tooling and infrastructure investments.

### Cross-Functional & Executive Partnership
- Serves as the primary engineering voice at the executive level, contributing to company strategy and planning.
- Partners closely with the Chief Product Officer, Chief Technology Officer (where applicable), and other C-suite leaders.
- Ensures that engineering is a trusted and well-understood function across the business.
- Communicates engineering strategy, performance, and risks clearly to the board and investors when required.

### Talent Strategy & Organizational Design
- Defines the structure of the engineering organization and evolves it as the company grows.
- Owns senior engineering hiring, including Directors, Principal Engineers, and other key roles.
- Develops the company's engineering employer brand to attract top talent.
- Creates clear career pathways and leveling frameworks that enable long-term retention and growth.

---

## Key Skills & Competencies

- Proven organizational leader; experienced building and scaling engineering teams through different stages of company growth.
- Deep technical credibility; able to engage substantively on architecture, infrastructure, and technical strategy.
- Exceptional communicator; able to present engineering clearly to boards, investors, and non-technical executives.
- Strong business acumen; understands how engineering decisions translate to commercial outcomes.
- Skilled at long-range planning and at making consequential decisions under uncertainty.
- Culturally influential; sets the tone for how engineering as a discipline is practiced and valued at the company.

## What Success Looks Like

- The engineering organization consistently delivers high-quality work that advances company goals.
- Engineering is viewed by the rest of the company as a strategic asset, not just a delivery function.
- The organization attracts and retains exceptional engineers at all levels.
- Technical foundations are sound — the company can move quickly without accumulating unsustainable risk.
- Engineering Directors and senior leaders are capable, well-developed, and effective.
- The engineering organization scales gracefully as the company grows, without losing quality or culture.
- Executive leadership has confidence in engineering's direction, capacity, and judgment.

## Comparison with Director of Software Engineering

The VP and the Director both lead above the team level, but the VP operates with greater scope, longer time horizons, and deeper involvement in company-wide strategy.

| | VP of Engineering | Director of Engineering |
|---|---|---|
| **Scope** | The entire engineering organization | An engineering department or functional area |
| **Manages** | Directors of Engineering | Engineering Managers (and sometimes senior ICs) |
| **Planning horizon** | Annual and multi-year | Multi-quarter |
| **Strategic role** | Defines and owns engineering strategy | Executes on engineering strategy; contributes to its definition |
| **External visibility** | Frequently represents engineering to board, investors, and partners | Occasionally represents engineering externally |
| **Organizational design** | Designs the broader engineering org structure | Adjusts team structures within their area |

The Director ensures that engineering teams are running well and aligned with current priorities. The VP sets the long-term direction for engineering and is accountable for the organization as a whole.
