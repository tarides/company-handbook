# Director of Software Engineering

## Role Overview

The Director of Software Engineering is responsible for the performance and direction of multiple engineering teams. Where an Engineering Manager focuses on a single team, the Director operates at the level of a broader engineering function — overseeing several managers, aligning teams around shared goals, and ensuring that engineering as a whole is working effectively toward the company's product and business objectives. The Director is both a people leader and a strategic contributor, and is accountable for the sustained health and output of their engineering organization.

---

## Responsibilities

### Organizational Leadership
- Leads and develops a group of Engineering Managers, providing coaching, feedback, and clear expectations.
- Builds a healthy, high-performing engineering organization with a strong culture of ownership and continuous improvement.
- Identifies and addresses organizational gaps, whether in structure, staffing, skills, or ways of working.
- Champions diversity, inclusion, and psychological safety across teams.

### Delivery & Operational Excellence
- Maintains visibility across all teams under their purview, identifying risks and systemic delivery issues before they escalate.
- Holds engineering managers accountable for their teams' delivery commitments and quality standards.
- Drives consistency in engineering practices, standards, and processes across teams.
- Identifies and removes organizational-level blockers that individual managers cannot resolve on their own.

### Strategic Planning & Roadmap Alignment
- Participates in product and business planning at a senior level, ensuring engineering capacity and capabilities are accurately represented.
- Translates company and product strategy into an engineering organizational plan, including headcount, team structure, and capability investments.
- Makes resourcing decisions across teams — allocating people and priorities in response to shifting business needs.
- Maintains a multi-quarter view of engineering work, balancing feature delivery with investment in infrastructure, tooling, and technical health.

### Technical Direction
- Ensures engineering teams are making sound architectural and technical decisions that scale appropriately with the business.
- Partners with senior engineers and tech leads to define and uphold engineering standards across the organization.
- Monitors and manages technical risk at an organizational level, ensuring it is understood and addressed over time.
- Stays sufficiently current on the technical landscape to evaluate build vs. buy decisions, tooling investments, and hiring needs.

### Hiring & Team Building
- Owns headcount planning and hiring for the engineering organization in partnership with HR and Finance.
- Actively participates in senior engineering hiring, including defining role requirements and making final decisions.
- Develops career pathways and progression frameworks to retain strong engineers and reward growth.

### Stakeholder Communication & Influence
- Communicates engineering status, risks, and strategic direction clearly to executive leadership and cross-functional partners.
- Builds trust and credibility with product, design, and business stakeholders through consistent follow-through and transparency.
- Represents the engineering organization in leadership discussions, advocating for the team's needs and priorities.

---

## Key Skills & Competencies

- Experienced people leader; able to lead managers and develop organizational capability, not just individual contributors.
- Strategic thinker; able to connect engineering decisions to business outcomes over a multi-quarter horizon.
- Clear communicator; comfortable presenting to senior leadership and synthesizing complex situations concisely.
- Technically credible; able to engage in substantive conversations about architecture, trade-offs, and technical risk.
- Strong organizational instincts; able to design team structures and processes that scale.
- Skilled at navigating competing priorities and making resourcing trade-offs with limited information.

## What Success Looks Like

- Engineering teams consistently deliver high-quality work and meet their commitments.
- Engineering Managers in the organization are growing, performing well, and well-supported.
- The engineering organization is structured appropriately for the company's current stage and near-term needs.
- Technical risk and debt are visible, understood, and being addressed in a planned way.
- Engineering has a strong reputation across the company as a reliable, high-quality partner.
- Headcount and resources are allocated to the highest-value work at any given time.

## Comparison with Engineering Manager

The Director and the Engineering Manager are both people leaders within the engineering organization, but they operate at different levels of scope and abstraction.

| | Director of Engineering | Engineering Manager |
|---|---|---|
| **Scope** | Multiple teams and managers | A single engineering team |
| **Manages** | Engineering Managers | Individual contributors (engineers) |
| **Planning horizon** | Multi-quarter organizational and strategic planning | Sprint and quarterly team planning |
| **Primary accountability** | Health and output of the engineering organization | Health and output of a single team |
| **Technical involvement** | Architectural direction and technical risk oversight | Day-to-day technical guidance and standards |
| **Hiring focus** | Headcount planning and senior hiring | Team-level hiring and onboarding |

The Engineering Manager is responsible for one team going well. The Director is responsible for an entire function going well — including the managers who run each team.

---

## Comparison with VP of Software Engineering

The Director and the VP both provide leadership above the team level, but the VP operates with greater scope, longer time horizons, and deeper involvement in company-wide strategy.

| | Director of Engineering | VP of Engineering |
|---|---|---|
| **Scope** | An engineering department or functional area | The entire engineering organization |
| **Manages** | Engineering Managers (and sometimes senior ICs) | Directors of Engineering |
| **Planning horizon** | Multi-quarter | Annual and multi-year |
| **Strategic role** | Executes on engineering strategy; contributes to its definition | Defines and owns engineering strategy |
| **External visibility** | Occasionally represents engineering externally | Frequently represents engineering to board, investors, and partners |
| **Organizational design** | Adjusts team structures within their area | Designs the broader engineering org structure |

The Director ensures that engineering teams are running well and aligned with current priorities. The VP sets the long-term direction for engineering and is accountable for the organization as a whole.
