# Software Engineer

## Role Overview

The Software Engineer designs, builds, tests, and maintains
software that serves the company's customers and advances its
technical goals. Software Engineers are the primary builders in
the organization. The role exists at multiple levels — junior
through principal — and while expectations of independence,
scope, and leadership grow significantly across those levels,
the core responsibilities are consistent. What changes is the
scale at which they are exercised, the degree to which they are
carried out without guidance, and the extent to which the
engineer raises the capability of those around them.

---

## Responsibilities

### Design & Implementation

- Designs and implements correct, readable, appropriately
  simple solutions.
- Breaks down requirements into well-scoped tasks, surfacing
  risks and open questions before work begins.
- Makes sound decisions about architecture and implementation
  within their area of work.
- Accounts for operational concerns — performance,
  reliability, observability, deployability — as part of
  design.

### Quality & Testing

- Takes personal responsibility for the quality of their
  output, including writing tests that give genuine confidence
  in correctness.
- Participates in code review, giving feedback that is
  specific, constructive, and focused on code health.
- Identifies and addresses fragility, inconsistency, and
  unnecessary complexity in the codebase.
- Treats bugs and incidents as signals about systemic issues,
  not just problems to close.

### Collaboration & Communication

- Works effectively with teammates, the Product Manager, and
  UX to understand and deliver against requirements.
- Communicates progress, blockers, and technical concerns
  clearly and promptly.
- Asks for help when stuck; offers help without waiting to be
  asked.
- Documents decisions and systems in a way that reduces
  single-person dependencies.

### Technical Ownership

- Takes genuine ownership of the areas of the codebase they
  work in, leaving them better than they found them.
- Monitors the health of systems they are responsible for and
  responds to issues promptly.
- Identifies and advocates for addressing technical debt that
  creates risk or slows the team.
- Follows through on commitments and communicates proactively
  when something is at risk.

### Growth & Contribution

- Actively develops their technical skills and domain
  knowledge.
- Shares knowledge through documentation, code review,
  pairing, and informal conversation.
- Contributes to a team culture of quality, learning, and
  honest feedback.
- Identifies opportunities to improve the team's tools and
  processes, and follows through on them.

---

## Key Skills & Competencies

- Proficiency in the languages, frameworks, and tools relevant
  to the team's work.
- Ability to reason clearly about software design, trade-offs,
  and correctness.
- Strong communication skills; able to explain technical ideas
  to both technical and non-technical colleagues.
- Thorough by habit; takes quality seriously without needing
  to be reminded.
- Collaborative by default; treats the team's success as more
  important than individual output.
- Intellectually curious; stays engaged with the field and
  brings relevant learning back to the team.

---

## Interfaces With

| Role | Nature of Interaction |
| --- | --- |
| Engineering Team | Daily collaboration on design, implementation, and review |
| Team Lead / Engineering Manager | Direction, feedback, and career support |
| Product Manager | Understanding requirements, raising questions, communicating progress |
| UX / Design | Implementing interfaces and providing feasibility input |
| Other Engineering Teams | Coordination on shared systems, APIs, and cross-cutting concerns |
| Customer Support | Occasional input on bugs, edge cases, and user-reported issues |

---

## What Success Looks Like

- Ships work that is correct, well-crafted, and delivered
  within a reasonable timeframe.
- Writes code that teammates find easy to understand and build
  on.
- Is a net positive on team morale — helpful, reliable, and
  honest.
- Leaves owned areas of the codebase healthier over time.
- Is visibly growing — taking on greater complexity, requiring
  less guidance, contributing more broadly.
- People seek out their input because they trust their
  judgment and find their feedback useful.
